1. The Cartographer’s Question

By the time Elias learned to read the creases in a map like a lover reads a face, his hands smelled of salt and old ink…

In a small port where gulls left secrets on rooftops, Elias met a captain who owned a ship stitched from glass and whale-bone—the Glass-Sailed, a vessel that caught starlight and folded it into sails.

The captain said only one thing that mattered:

“We go where the compass forgets.”
